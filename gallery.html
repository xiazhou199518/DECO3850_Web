    • Game Endings

    Since we believe that the most suitable method help people deal with environmental challenges might be to allow them make own decisions via self-exploration. That is reason why the game should be open-end. The different endings of the game be triggered according to players’ actions and decisions during the game.

    Millionaire

    Triggered by values in high profits & low pollution

    Environment Protector

    Triggered by values in medium profits & very low pollution

    Environment Destroyed

    Triggered by values in high pollution

    Bankruptcy

    Triggered by values in very low profits

    Reflection on Individual Contribution

    Reviewing this semester, DECO3850 provides a great trip and experience for me. It is a hectic and heavy course because it worths 4 units and expects students to spend more time and efforts on it. Compared with other design courses, I think that it is more integrity and comprehensive so it requires higher standard of design skills and knowledge. In other words, this course nearly not only combines all of the knowledge I learned from previous courses, but also teach me about physical computing & interactive design. For example, when my team was developing our game project, we needed skills on software programing (CSSE2002), digital prototyping (DECO2300), graphic design (DECO2200) and HCI (DECO2500) etc. Technically, we needed C#, Unity 3D, Adobe Illustrator and Premiere etc. Of course, repeating these skills should not be the key tasks to me, so I must try something new and learn & practice from that. This is the first time, I was involved in building the physical installation manually and then demonstrating the product to the public at Exhibit. This makes me feel like I am being closer to my dream -- become a designer.
